Accumulation Phase
The period during which you pay premiums or invest money into an annuity.
Accumulation Value
The total amount of money in an annuity during the accumulation phase.
Annuitant
The person whose life expectancy is used to determine the timing and amount of annuity payments.
Annuitization
The process of converting an annuity investment into a series of periodic income payments.
Annuitization Options
Various methods by which annuity payments can be structured, such as life income, life with period certain, joint life, etc.
Annuity
A financial product that provides a series of payments made at equal intervals, used primarily as a means of securing a steady cash flow during retirement.
Bailout Provision
A feature in some annuities that allows the policyholder to withdraw funds without penalty if the interest rate credited drops below a specified level.
Beneficiary
The person or entity designated to receive any remaining payments or death benefit upon the annuitant’s death.
Deferred Annuity
An annuity in which payments begin at a future date.
Distribution Phase
The period during which the annuity makes periodic payments to the annuitant.
Equity-Indexed Annuity
An annuity that earns interest based on changes in a stock market index, which can provide higher returns with some level of risk protection.
Fixed Annuity
An annuity that pays a guaranteed rate of interest.
Fixed Indexed Annuity
An annuity that credits interest based on the performance of a specified stock market index.
Free-Look Period
A time period during which a new annuity policyholder can cancel the policy without penalties.
Guaranteed Lifetime Withdrawal Benefit (GLWB)
An annuity feature that guarantees the annuitant can withdraw a certain amount each year for life, even if the account balance drops to zero.
Immediate Annuity
An annuity that starts payments almost immediately after a lump-sum premium is paid.
Income Rider
An optional feature that can be added to an annuity to provide a guaranteed stream of income.
Longevity Annuity
An annuity that starts payments at a later age, typically after 80, to provide income for the remaining years of life.
Market Value Adjustment (MVA)
A feature in some annuities that adjusts the surrender value based on changes in interest rates.
Mortality and Expense Risk Charge
A fee that covers the insurance company's risks in providing the annuity, including the risk that the annuitant will live longer than expected.
Non-Qualified Annuity
An annuity funded with after-tax dollars that is not part of a qualified retirement plan.
Period Certain
An option in an annuity that guarantees payments for a specific number of years, even if the annuitant dies.
Periodic Payments
Payments made regularly (monthly, quarterly, annually) from an annuity.
Qualified Longevity Annuity Contract (QLAC)
A deferred income annuity purchased within a retirement account that begins payments at an advanced age and meets certain IRS requirements.
Rider
An add-on to a standard insurance policy that provides additional benefits or coverage.
Single Premium Immediate Annuity (SPIA)
An annuity purchased with a single premium that starts making payments almost immediately.
Surrender Charge
A fee you may have to pay if you withdraw money from an annuity before a certain period.
Surrender Charge Period
The time during which a withdrawal from an annuity will incur a surrender charge.
Surrender Value
The amount available in cash upon cancellation of an annuity before it starts paying benefits.
Variable Annuity
An annuity that allows you to invest in various sub-accounts, which are similar to mutual funds. The value of the annuity can go up or down based on the performance of the investments.
Vesting
The process by which a policyholder earns the right to receive benefits from a retirement plan or an annuity.
Waiver of Premium Rider
A feature that allows the policyholder to stop paying premiums if they become seriously ill or disabled.
